DIANA
Frances Diana Spencer
July 1, 1961 — August 31, 1997

01 — CHILDHOOD
The girl nobody expected
Diana Spencer was born July 1, 1961 at Park House, Norfolk. Her parents desperately wanted a son. She was their third daughter — unexpected. When her parents divorced she was 8. She stood at the window watching her mother's car disappear. It never came back. She failed her school exams twice — not from lack of intelligence, but from a broken belief that she was worth anything at all.
1961 — 1977
02 — THE WEDDING
She knew. She married him anyway.
The night before the wedding Diana found cufflinks on Charles — engraved with interlinked C's. A gift from Camilla Parker Bowles. His lover. He wore them on their wedding day. Diana cried for days before the ceremony. She walked down the aisle red-eyed, terrified. 750 million people watched and called it a fairy tale. She was 20 years old. She had known Charles for 13 encounters. She later said it was the worst day of her life.
July 29, 1981
03 — THE PALACE PRISON
A bird in a gilded cage
Inside the Palace, Diana's friends were cut off. Her movements monitored. Her letters reviewed. She attempted to take her own life 5 times. The Palace kept every incident quiet. The Queen never once asked if she was okay. In 1994 Charles publicly admitted his affair with Camilla on television. Diana watched from a private room. One year later — Panorama. 23 million viewers. 'There were three of us in this marriage. So it was a bit crowded.' The Palace never forgave her. The divorce was finalized August 28, 1996. They stripped her of her HRH title.
1992 — 1996
04 — THE LAST SUMMER
For the first time — she was free
Summer 1997. Mediterranean. Diana was finally herself again. Dodi Fayed — son of Mohamed Al Fayed — entered her life through mutual friends. Their relationship grew quietly through July and August 1997. Close friends said she was happier than she had been in years. She was planning her future. She was 36 years old. She had time. What she did not know: she had 30 days left.
Summer 1997
05 — PONT DE L'ALMA
August 31, 1997. 12:23 AM.
The Mercedes entered the tunnel being chased by 7 paparazzi motorcycles. The driver's blood alcohol: 3x legal limit. The crash happened at 12:23 AM. Diana was alive when rescuers arrived. The ambulance took 43 minutes to reach a hospital 4 miles away. She died at 4:00 AM. Mohamed Al Fayed maintained for years it was no accident. Two official investigations said it was. What remains undeniable: a woman was being chased at high speed by people whose income depended on her photograph. The culture that created the demand was never put on trial.
August 31, 1997 — 4:00 AM
06 — THE WORLD SAID GOODBYE
1 billion people mourned a stranger
September 6, 1997. Streets of London lined with flowers — millions thrown by people who had never met her but felt they had lost someone they deeply loved. Her sons walked behind her coffin. William was 15. Harry was 12. The world watched two boys bury their mother and called it a state occasion. 1 billion people watched the funeral — the largest television audience in human history.
September 6, 1997
07 — THE LETTER
She wrote that she would be killed
Ten months before the crash, Diana wrote a letter to her butler Paul Burrell — her most trusted friend. In it she wrote: 'My husband is planning an accident in my car — brake failure and serious head injury — in order to clear the way for him to marry.' The letter was revealed in 2003. The British establishment called it paranoia. Make of that what you will.
October 1996 — revealed 2003
